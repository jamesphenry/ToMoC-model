NEITHER trained on it). Removes the data-shift confound from the prior
200ep-overfit-probe (which compared 200ep-val vs 100ep-OLD-full-set).

## VERDICT (CONCLUSIVE)
200ep HURT. 100ep is the sweet spot.
- 60->100ep: FIXED undertraining (get_time 0->66), +27 route_acc. GOOD.
- 100->200ep: REGRESSED. val route_acc 88.5->70.5 (-18). under_call
  0->21% (model got reluctant to emit tools). Classic EPOCH OVERKILL on a
  tiny model: past ~100ep it fits training quirks, loses tool/no-tool edge.
- SWEET SPOT = 100ep / 2.3M. Stop pushing epochs here.

## LESSON
"Push scoring without overfitting" -> there IS a ceiling from epochs alone,
and it's BELOW where loss stops improving (train loss 0.0225 @200ep looked
great, but val told the truth). The sealed val split is what caught it.
Never trust train loss / train-acc on a tiny from-scratch model as the
stopping signal — hold out data.

## Answer to user's Q ("push without overfitting?")
Pushed 60->100 (helped), 100->200 (overfit/regressed). Found the cliff.
Best score on held-out: 100ep / 2.3M @ 88.5% val route_acc, 0% under_call.
That is the model. Next gains require fixing the tool/no-tool BOUNDARY
(under_call appeared at 200ep; even at 100ep over_call 6.6% = emits tool
when it should answer_direct), NOT more epochs or bigger arch.
